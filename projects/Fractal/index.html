Some Info about my DRP Presentation!

In the winter quarter of 2023, I worked with a mathematics graduate student, John G. Pelias, to research and complete a project surrounding the dimension of chaotic attractors. At the end of the quarter, this culminated into a 15 minute presentation. The title of the presentation is "Boxes and Balls: Quantifying Chaos".

Throughout the process, I was working on developing MatLab code in order to numerically compute the definitions of dimension which I had studied in the efforts of the project. Results from these limited numerical computations are at the end of the presentation. Ultimately, I learned a lot about self-directed research and some of the difficulties of numerical computation.

Some of the more challenging concepts which I had to learn in this project include:

  • The Variational Equation for Dynamical Systems
  • Gram-Schmidt Ortho-normalization Numerical Procedure for n-dim vectors
  • Definitions of Fractal dimension
    • Box-Counting/Capacity Dimension
    • Lyapunov Dimension
    • Renyi Dimension
    • Pointwise Dimension
  • Numerical Computation of Lyapunov Exponents
